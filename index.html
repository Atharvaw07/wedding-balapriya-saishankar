The Rituals

Viratham

The wedding ceremony begins with Ganapati Puja, followed by Vratham performed separately for the bride and groom. For the bride, the tying of the Kaapu symbolizes divine protection, while for the groom, it marks his transition from bachelorhood to family life.

Nichaya Muhurtham

During the Nitchayathaartham, the bride and groom’s families formally come together to bless the couple and announce their upcoming wedding. The ceremony includes the exchange of rings, reading of the wedding agreement (Lagna Pathirikai), and seeking elders’ blessings, symbolizing love, commitment, and the beginning of a lifelong journey together.

Radha Kalyanam

This ceremony celebrates the divine love and eternal romance between Lord Krishna and Radha through the singing of Ashtapadi hymns (Ashta – eight, Padi – steps). The verses portray their longing, separation, deep affection, and joyful reunion, blessing the bride and groom with everlasting love, harmony, and togetherness in their married life.

Kasi Yathra

In this light-hearted ritual, the groom pretends to renounce worldly life and leave for Kasi carrying an umbrella and walking stick. The bride’s father then persuades him to return and accept the responsibilities of married life.

Maalai Maatral

During this joyful ritual, the bride and groom exchange garlands while being lifted by their respective uncles. This symbolizes the union of two souls coming together in love and companionship.

Oonjal

The bride and groom are seated on a beautifully decorated swing (Oonjal) while the women of the family sing Laali songs to bless the couple. The gentle back-and-forth motion of the swing reflects the ups and downs of life, reminding the couple to remain united, balanced, and harmonious through every stage of their journey together.

Kanya Dhanam

Kanya Dhanam is one of the most sacred rituals of the wedding, where the bride’s parents formally offer their daughter’s hand in marriage to the groom. It symbolizes their love, blessings, and trust as they entrust her happiness, care, and future to her life partner.

Mangalya Dharanam

Mangalya Dharanam is the most cherished moment of the wedding ceremony, where the groom ties the sacred Mangalyam around the bride’s neck amidst the chanting of Vedic hymns. This ritual signifies their eternal bond, mutual commitment, and the beginning of a joyful and blessed married life together.

Paani Grahanam

Paani Grahanam is a sacred ritual where the groom gently holds the bride’s hand, expressing his promise to stand by her through every stage of life. It reflects mutual trust, support, and the beginning of their journey together as equal partners in marriage.

Saptapadhi

Sapthapadhi (Saptha – seven, Padhi – steps) is one of the most important rituals of a Hindu wedding, where the bride and groom take seven sacred steps together around the holy fire. Each step represents a vow of love, trust, prosperity, strength, happiness, family, and lifelong togetherness.
